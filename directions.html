About Siniloan

Siniloan is located between the plains of the Sierra Madre Mountains and Laguna de Bay. It is bordered by the municipality of Mabitac to the west, Pañguil to the east, Real, Quezon to the north, and Laguna de Bay to the south. The town of Fámy, once a small barrio of Siniloan known as Barrio Calumpang, became a separate municipality in 1910, while Mabitac, also formerly a barrio of Sinilóan, was separated much earlier in 1613. Flowing through the center of the town is the Río Romelo, which serves as both a fishing ground and a source of irrigation. The town proper is situated about 84 kilometers (52 miles) from Manila via Manila East Road or Marcos Highway, and roughly 113 kilometers (70 miles) via the South Luzon Expressway through Santa Cruz.
