Discover Siniloan’s Food

Siniloan’s culinary heritage shines through its beloved treats. Buko Pie, a flaky pastry filled with tender young coconut, offers a sweet and creamy indulgence. Cassava Cake, made from grated cassava and coconut milk, delights with its soft, chewy texture and golden top. Kesong Puti, a fresh and slightly salty white cheese, reflects the town’s farming traditions and pairs perfectly with local bread. To complete the feast, Sinukmani, a sticky rice cake cooked in coconut milk and sugar, is often shared during gatherings as a symbol of togetherness — and many more delights await, such as Uraro cookies, best discovered in the local market or on our Food page.
